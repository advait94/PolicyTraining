DPDP Act - Rights of Data Principals

DPDP Act - Obligations of Data Fiduciaries

Data Fiduciaries must ensure compliance with the Act, similar to GDPR's accountability principle.

Consent (Chapter II):

Processing of personal data generally requires the consent of the Data Principal for a specified purpose.

Consent must be free, specific, informed, unambiguous, and indicate clear affirmative action.

Data Principal must be provided with a notice describing the personal data to be collected and the purpose of processing.

The Data Principal has the right to withdraw consent at any time.

Legitimate Uses (Deemed Consent) (Chapter II, Section 7):

The Act outlines certain "legitimate uses" where consent is not required (deemed consent). These include:

For a legal obligation on the Data Fiduciary.

For response to medical emergency involving threat to life or health.

For providing a service or benefit to the Data Principal that they had requested.

For employment purposes.

In public interest (e.g., preventing fraud, mergers & acquisitions).

Data Minimization & Accuracy (Chapter II):

Data Fiduciaries must make reasonable efforts to ensure the accuracy and completeness of personal data, and erase data if it's no longer necessary for the stated purpose.

Only collect data necessary for the purpose.

Security Safeguards (Chapter II, Section 8):

Data Fiduciaries must implement reasonable security safeguards to prevent personal data breach. This includes technical and organizational measures.

Personal Data Breach Notification (Chapter II, Section 8):

In the event of a personal data breach, the Data Fiduciary must notify the Data Protection Board of India and affected Data Principals in case of a breach affecting Data Principals in India. The rules will specify the timeline and manner of notification.

Obligations in relation to Data Processors (Chapter II, Section 9):

Data Fiduciaries are responsible for ensuring that Data Processors engaged by them also comply with the provisions of the Act.

Processing by a Data Processor must be under a valid contract with the Data Fiduciary.

Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIA) & Data Auditor (Chapter IV):

The Act mentions the concept of "Significant Data Fiduciaries" (SDFs) based on criteria like volume, sensitivity of data, risk to rights, and potential impact.

SDFs may be required to undertake DPIAs, appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO), and conduct independent data audits. Rules will specify criteria for SDFs.

Grievance Redressal Mechanism (Chapter III):

Data Fiduciaries must establish an effective and accessible grievance redressal mechanism for Data Principals.
