Data Protection Board of India & Penalties

DPDP Act - Rights of Data Principals

The DPDP Act grants individuals significant rights over their personal data, empowering them with control and access.

Right to Information about Personal Data (Chapter III, Section 10):

Data Principals have the right to obtain a summary of personal data being processed, information about processing activities, identities of Data Fiduciaries/Processors, and categories of personal data processed.

This is typically provided via a comprehensive privacy notice or policy.

Right to Access Personal Data (Chapter III, Section 11):

Data Principals can request access to their personal data, along with information about its processing.

This enables individuals to verify accuracy and compliance.

Right to Correction & Erasure of Personal Data (Chapter III, Section 12):

Data Principals have the right to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data.

They also have the right to request erasure of their personal data if it is no longer necessary for the purpose for which it was collected.

Right to Grievance Redressal (Chapter III, Section 13):

Data Principals have the right to have their grievances redressed by the Data Fiduciary.

If unsatisfied with the Data Fiduciary's response, they can appeal to the Data Protection Board of India.

Right to Nominate (Chapter III, Section 14):

A Data Principal has the right to nominate another individual who will exercise their rights under the Act in the event of their death or incapacity.
