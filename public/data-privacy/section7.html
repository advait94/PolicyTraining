DPDP Act - Obligations of Data Fiduciaries

Introduction to the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act) - India

Context and Objective

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP Act) is India's first comprehensive and unified law specifically governing the processing of digital personal data.

It was enacted in August 2023, following years of deliberation, and once fully enforced (after rules are notified), it will largely supersede earlier data protection provisions under the Information Technology Act, 2000.

Primary Aim: To provide for the processing of digital personal data in a manner that recognizes both the right of individuals (Data Principals) to protect their personal data and the need to process such data for lawful purposes.

It seeks to balance individual rights with legitimate business needs and national interests.

Key Definitions (DPDP Act vs. GDPR)

Data Principal: The individual to whom the personal data relates. (Equivalent to GDPR's "Data Subject").

Data Fiduciary: An entity (person or organization) who alone or in conjunction with other persons determines the purpose and means of processing personal data. (Equivalent to GDPR's "Data Controller"). Our company will primarily act as a Data Fiduciary.

Data Processor: An entity that processes personal data on behalf of a Data Fiduciary. (Similar to GDPR's "Data Processor").

Personal Data: Any data about an individual who is identifiable by or in relation to such data.

Processing: An automated operation or set of operations performed on digital personal data, including collection, storage, use, alteration, retrieval, sharing, disclosure, erasure, or destruction.

Consent: Defined as clear, affirmative action, freely given, specific, informed, and unambiguous.

Applicability and Scope

Applies to:

Processing of digital personal data within India.

Processing of digital personal data outside India if it is for offering goods or services to Data Principals in India.

Exclusions:

Offline personal data (unless digitized subsequently).

Non-personal data.

Personal data processed for domestic or personal purposes.

Processing of personal data for journalistic purposes (subject to regulations).

Territorial Reach: The Act has extraterritorial application, meaning it applies to entities outside India if they process personal data of Data Principals in India for specific purposes (e.g., offering goods/services).
