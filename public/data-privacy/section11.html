Our Company's Responsibilities & Your Role

International Data Transfers under DPDP Act

General Rule (Chapter IV, Section 16)

The DPDP Act allows for the transfer of personal data outside India, except to such countries or territories as may be restricted by the Central Government by notification.

This signifies an "allow by default, restrict by exception" approach, unlike GDPR's "restrict by default, allow by exception" approach.

The Central Government will issue rules specifying the conditions for such transfers, which might include standard contractual clauses or specific assessments.

Implications for Cross-Border Data Flows

While the initial framework appears more liberal than GDPR, the actual implementation will depend on the rules notified by the Central Government.

Organizations must stay updated on the notified list of restricted countries and any specific mechanisms (e.g., contractual clauses, certifications) that may be prescribed for transfers to other countries.

For companies dealing with both EU and Indian data, ensuring compliance with both GDPR and DPDP Act requirements for international transfers will be crucial, often meaning adhering to the stricter of the two.
