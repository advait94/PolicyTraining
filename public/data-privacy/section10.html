Data Protection Board of India & Penalties

Data Protection Board of India (Chapter V)

The Act establishes the Data Protection Board of India (DPBI).

Role:

An independent body responsible for enforcing the provisions of the DPDP Act.

Inquiring into personal data breaches and non-compliance.

Imposing penalties.

Directing Data Fiduciaries to take necessary measures.

Advising the Central Government on data protection matters.

The Board will have powers similar to a civil court.

Penalties for Non-Compliance (Chapter VI)

The DPDP Act imposes significant penalties for various forms of non-compliance, with maximum penalties much higher than previous Indian data protection laws.

Failure to take reasonable security safeguards to prevent personal data breach: Up to INR 250 Crore.

Failure to notify the Board and affected Data Principals of a personal data breach: Up to INR 200 Crore.

Failure to fulfill obligations for children's data: Up to INR 200 Crore.

Failure of Significant Data Fiduciary to perform duties (DPIA, DPO, audit): Up to INR 150 Crore.

Failure to comply with obligations regarding data principals' rights: Up to INR 100 Crore.

Any other non-compliance: Penalties as prescribed in the Schedule.

Key Considerations for Penalties

The penalty amount will be decided by the Data Protection Board, considering factors like the nature, gravity, and duration of the breach, the type and sensitivity of personal data involved, and whether the Data Fiduciary took steps to mitigate the consequences.

Penalties are intended to be "effective, proportionate, and dissuasive."
