Your Role & Responsibilities in Cybersecurity - BE OUR HUMAN FIREWALL!

Our Company's Information Security Policies

These policies are your guide. Understanding and following them is not optional; it's mandatory.

Our Commitment to Your Security

Our company has developed and implemented a comprehensive set of Information Security Policies designed to protect our valuable assets, ensure regulatory compliance (Indian and global), and maintain a secure working environment for everyone.

These policies define acceptable use of our IT resources, outline security best practices, and detail procedures for handling sensitive information.

Key Policies You Must Be Aware Of:

Information Security Policy:

What it is: Our overarching document outlining the company's commitment to information security, its objectives, scope, and general principles (like the CIA Triad). It forms the foundation of our security program.

Your Action!: Read and understand this foundational document to grasp our security philosophy.

Acceptable Use Policy (AUP):

What it is: Defines the rules for using company IT resources (computers, networks, internet, email, software, mobile devices).

Covers: Prohibitions on unauthorized software installation, accessing inappropriate content, misuse of company assets, and activities that could compromise security (e.g., peer-to-peer sharing on company network).

Your Action!: Know what you can and cannot do with company IT resources. Misuse can lead to disciplinary action.

Data Classification & Handling Policy:

What it is: Details how different types of data (Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted/Highly Confidential) must be labeled, stored, accessed, and transmitted. It also specifies procedures for secure data disposal.

Your Action!: Learn how to classify the data you work with and follow the specific handling requirements for each classification level. Incorrect handling of sensitive data is a common cause of breaches.

Remote Work & Mobile Device Security Policy:

What it is: Provides clear guidelines for securely working from home or any remote location. It also outlines rules for using company-issued or personal devices for work (BYOD - Bring Your Own Own Device), if permitted.

Covers: Requirements for device encryption, strong passwords, VPN usage for remote access, securing your home network, and protecting physical devices outside the office.

Your Action!: If you work remotely or use mobile devices for work, this policy is essential for your compliance.

Password Policy:

What it is: Specific requirements for creating and managing passwords (length, complexity, rotation if applicable, and non-reuse). It explicitly mandates the use of Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).

Your Action!: This policy directly impacts your daily login routine. Follow it precisely.

Incident Response Policy:

What it is: Outlines the structured procedures for identifying, reporting, containing, eradicating, and recovering from security incidents. It details roles, responsibilities, and communication protocols during an incident.

Your Action!: While the IT/Security team leads this, knowing your role in reporting (as discussed on Slide 10) is critical for its success.

Social Media Policy:

What it is: Provides guidelines for professional conduct on social media platforms, especially when representing the company or discussing work-related topics.

Emphasizes: Not sharing confidential company or customer information online, avoiding defamatory statements, and respecting privacy.

Your Action!: Be mindful of your digital footprint and remember that your online actions can impact the company's reputation and security.

Where to Access Our Policies

All company policies are readily available on the [Company Intranet / HR Portal / Dedicated Policy Document Repository Link].

It is mandatory for all employees to read, understand, and adhere to these policies.

Seek clarification from [HR / IT / Legal / Designated Security Contact] if you have any questions or are unsure about any aspect of these policies. Don't guess; ask!
